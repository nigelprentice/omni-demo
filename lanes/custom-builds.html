Honest scope

What $700K–$3M actually buys — and what it doesn't.

The number on a custom builder's "starting at" page rarely survives the first 30 days. Here's the honest band, with what's in and what isn't, before you ever sit down with us.

$700K – $1.2M

Foundation custom

~3,500–4,500 sf · 1+ acre lot

  • Architectural drawings + structural engineering
  • Three covered outdoor rooms (porches, terraces, kitchen)
  • Two-car attached garage
  • Mid-tier finish allowances, defined at contract
  • Standard pool deck (pool itself separate)
$1.2M – $2M

Signature custom

~4,500–6,000 sf · 1–5 acre lot

  • All Foundation scope, plus:
  • Negative-edge pool + outdoor entertaining program
  • Detached casita or guesthouse (~600–1,200 sf)
  • Wine cellar, home office, secondary suite
  • Upper-tier interior + exterior finish allowances
  • Smart-home + security base build
$2M – $3M+

Estate custom

~6,000+ sf · 2+ acre lot

  • All Signature scope, plus:
  • Detached guest house, pool house, or barndominium
  • Specialty rooms (gym, theater, wine room, in-home spa)
  • Site work for sloped or off-grid lots
  • Custom millwork + interior design package
  • Septic + water capture for unincorporated parcels
Not included in any band: land cost, lot survey + soil testing, landscape installation beyond grading, fine-art lighting, pool features beyond standard, HOA fees, financing costs. We list these explicitly in the contract so nothing gets to "true-up" at the end.
What happens after you book

Five phases. One team. One number.

Every milestone is documented in writing before the next one starts. You can audit any phase at any point.

  1. 01

    Discovery 1–2 weeks

    30-minute design consult, then a paid feasibility session if there's fit. We confirm lot status, lane, scope range, and whether you want to keep an existing architect's plans or design fresh with us.

  2. 02

    Design 4–8 weeks

    Schematic design, design development, and construction documents — all in-house. Selections start in week 3. We don't permit until allowances are locked.

  3. 03

    Permit + pre-con 4–12 weeks

    Submittal, HOA + jurisdiction review, value engineering if needed, and the locked-budget pre-construction meeting. No surprises after this point.

  4. 04

    Build 10–16 months

    Weekly written updates with photos. Same project lead every week. Site visits scheduled, not pushed. Change orders only when you initiate, only in writing.

  5. 05

    Handoff + warranty 1 week + 1 year

    30-day post-occupancy walkthrough. 1-year warranty visit. 48-hour response window for any warranty ticket — written into the contract.

Honest answers

The questions you've been asking other builders — and what we'd actually answer.

"My architect already drew plans. Can you just build it?" +

Yes — about 30% of our projects come in with existing plans, and we're glad to take them. We'll do a free plan review first to flag missing detail (flashing, load-support, blocking, mechanical chases) that only shows up later as change orders. If your plans are construction-ready, we go straight to pricing. If they need clean-up, we tell you exactly what's missing and what it will cost — before contract.

"How is your number going to compare to a GC + subs route?" +

In a fair apples-to-apples comparison — same square footage, same lot, same finish level — Omni typically comes in within 4–7% of a GC-plus-subs number on day one. The honest difference shows up at month 14: GC + subs builds in this area average 18–24% over their signing number. Ours don't, because the allowance exposure is closed before construction begins. The number you sign is the number you live with.

"I'm shopping three builders. Why should I include you?" +

We expect you to. Bid us against two others and ask each of them three questions: (1) Who is my single point of contact and is it the same person at month one and month sixteen? (2) When are allowances finalized, and what happens if a category goes over? (3) Show me the last three projects you delivered and the contract-vs-actual final number. The answers will tell you everything. We hand ours over before you ask.

"How long does this really take? Be honest." +

From signed contract to keys: 14–22 months for Foundation and Signature builds, 18–28 months for Estate. Design alone is 4–8 weeks. The variable is permit and HOA review (some Hill Country jurisdictions are predictable, some are not — we'll tell you which on the first call once we know your address). What we won't do is quote a 12-month build that we know is 18.

"What's your warranty? What actually happens if something fails?" +

One-year comprehensive on workmanship. Two years on systems (electrical, plumbing, HVAC). Ten years on structural. The line you actually care about: a 48-hour response window on every warranty ticket, written into the contract. If we miss it, you get a per-day credit. We've never had to issue one — and we'd rather keep it that way.

"I don't own land yet. Are we wasting your time talking?" +

No. We work with a small network of land brokers in Dripping Springs, Wimberley, Spicewood, Marble Falls, Driftwood, and the western Hill Country who understand what builds well and what doesn't. We'll do a 30-minute call, give you a list of what to look for (slope, septic, sight lines, access), and stay in touch until you've found something. No pressure, no contract needed at this stage.
