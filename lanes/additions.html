Why owners hire Omni for additions

Adding to a home shouldn't feel like starting from scratch.

Outdoor living that actually gets used.

The pool you don't use, the patio you don't sit on, the outdoor kitchen no one cooks in — those are the ones designed by someone who never asked how you actually live. We start with the question of what you want to be doing on a Saturday at 6 PM, then we build backward. Shade, breeze, sight lines, and step counts get engineered before the plumbing does.

Casitas that earn their keep.

Whether it's a guesthouse for visiting parents, a short-term rental, a home office, or a teenager's retreat — a casita has to work for the next twenty years, not just the next reunion. We design for flexibility: separate entry, separate utilities, separate climate, and a structure that ages with the main house instead of looking bolted on.

Primary suites built like they belong.

A primary suite addition fails when it reads as an afterthought — different roofline, different ceiling height, different light. We match the existing envelope, then push the ceiling up, the windows out, and the closet through. The result feels like the house was always supposed to be this way.

What $300K–$1M actually buys — and what it doesn't.

The "starting at" number on most addition pages rarely survives the first 30 days. Here's the honest band, with what's in and what isn't, before you sit down with us.

$300K – $500K

Foundation addition

~400–800 sf added · 4–6 month build

  • One major addition (suite, kitchen, or great room)
  • Architectural drawings + structural engineering
  • One covered outdoor room (porch or patio)

~1,500+ sf added · 9–14 month build

  • Custom millwork + interior design package
  • Septic/water expansion if needed
Not included in any band: permits + impact fees, lot survey + soil testing, landscape installation beyond grading, pool features beyond standard, HOA fees, financing costs. We list these explicitly in the contract so nothing gets to "true-up" at the end.

Honest answers

The questions we get on every first call — and the answers, straight.

"Can we live in the house during the build?" +

For most additions, yes — we set up a clean dust-wall separation, route subs through a single entry, and protect existing finishes. There are two cases where we recommend a short-term move-out: kitchen gut paired with primary suite gut (water and food at the same time is a hard combo), and any structural opening that requires temporary shoring. We'll tell you which on the first call.

"How does an addition affect property taxes?" +

Travis and Hays counties reassess based on the permit value and the new improvement. As a rule of thumb, expect the addition's reassessed value to be roughly proportional to the project cost, with the homestead cap (10%/year) limiting how fast it phases in. We'll give you a realistic estimate at design, and we recommend a quick call with your county appraisal district before signing — it's a free conversation that takes 15 minutes.

"Can you build a pool without the rest of the program?" +

Yes — and we will, if that's what fits. But we'll show you the cost of doing it as a standalone project versus doing it alongside the patio and outdoor kitchen you'll almost certainly want within 18 months. The labor and permit overhead doesn't double when you do them together — it gets amortized once. Most owners save 12–18% by bundling, and the design lines actually align.

"What's the realistic timeline from first call to swimming?" +

For a Foundation-band project: 4–7 months from contract to occupancy. For Signature: 6–10 months. For Estate: 9–14 months. The variable is permit + HOA review (some Hill Country jurisdictions are predictable, some aren't — we'll tell you which on the first call once we know your address). What we won't do is quote a 4-month build that we know is 7.

"What's your warranty on additions?" +

One-year comprehensive on workmanship. Two years on systems we install (electrical, plumbing, HVAC, pool equipment). Ten years on structural. The line you actually care about: 48-hour response window on every warranty ticket, written into the contract.

"Can you match an existing limestone or stucco?" +

Yes — we sample the existing material, identify the quarry or supplier where possible, and order from the same source. Where the original is no longer available, we tell you upfront and show two or three closest matches before signing. We'd rather have that conversation in week 1 than week 14.
